⚠️ How to Protect Yourself

📱 Social Media Scams

Fake Instagram vendors, Twitter giveaway scams, Facebook marketplace fraud. Always check a seller's handle before sending money.

💰 Investment Scams

Ponzi schemes, fake crypto platforms, "double your money" offers. If returns sound too good to be true — they are.

🏠 Rental Scams

Fake landlords collecting rent for property they don't own. Always verify ownership at the land registry before payment.

💼 Job Scams

Fake job offers requiring upfront payment for training, uniform, or processing fees. Legitimate employers do not charge to hire you.

🛡️ About TrustCheck Nigeria

TrustCheck Nigeria is a community-powered scam reporting and business verification platform built to protect Nigerians from online fraud.

Nigeria loses billions of naira every year to online scams — fake vendors on Instagram, investment fraud on WhatsApp, romance scams on Telegram, fake job offers, and Ponzi schemes. There was no single searchable place where Nigerians could instantly check if a phone number, business, or account had been reported. TrustCheck fills that gap.

What makes us different

Unlike government portals that only tell you if a business is registered, TrustCheck combines CAC government verification with community social proof — so you know not just if a business exists, but whether it's honest.

How the Trust Score works: Our score (0–100) is calculated using report count, unique reporters, community star ratings, and vote credibility. A business with 50 reports from 50 different people is treated very differently from one with 50 reports from the same account.

Verification: Businesses can apply for CAC and identity verification (NIN, BVN, Driver's License) via our partner APIs. Verified businesses receive a trust badge visible in all search results.
